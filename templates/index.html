"Due to fluctuating prices of natural gas and uncertainty in the future supply of electricity, heat and CO2, greenhouses in the West-Holland region are struggling to remain competitive with decreased security of supply, all whilst striving to meet the horticulture sector’s goal of becoming carbon neutral by 2040. Their infrastructure and behaviour must be adapted to meet these goals whilst remaining a profitable, and industry-leading sector. After this, the scope of the project is discussed and the various sustainability goals are mentioned."

The recommendations present the technologies which were found to be the easiest and cheapest to implement to satisfy the carbon-neutrality constraint of the problem. The highest energetic requirement, Heat is recommended to be covered by a small part of Biogas in CHP plants, dominant geothermal energy combined with thermal storage, and through the import of Harbour heat with provenance from WarmtelinQ and Europoort projects. Electricity is recommended to be supplied by low-cost investments in wind and solar power. Since heat supply is produced in part from biogas combustion, electricity is produced in equal parts and can be used to supplement the base-load electricity supply or exported to the grid (creating added value to the system). Finally, the required CO2 could partially be produced in CHP plants run on biogas. A second important source of CO2 is the OCAP pipeline, where it is recommended to increase their affinity and get a more optimized schedule of the CO2 supply by the source
